Take Charge of Your Heart: Know the Risks of High Blood Pressure

Blood pressure, often called the "silent killer," affects millions worldwide. Learning how it works is the first step to taking control of your health.

High blood pressure can quietly damage your arteries, heart, kidneys, and more—often without noticeable symptoms. But the good news is that it’s manageable with early awareness.

In this section, discover what high blood pressure is, why it matters, and how it impacts everyday life.

A visual showing a heartbeat waveform

Spot the Signs, Know the Triggers, Control the Pressure

Awareness leads to prevention. Learn the causes, symptoms, and how to stay in control.

What Triggers High Blood Pressure

  • Excessive salt or fat intake
  • Stress and anxiety
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Obesity and family history
  • Smoking and alcohol consumption

Speak with a medical professional to better understand your risks and lifestyle changes that help.

Warning Signs to Watch For

  • Frequent headaches
  • Dizziness or blurred vision
  • Shortness of breath
  • Nosebleeds
  • Fatigue or confusion

Don't ignore these signs—early action can prevent life-threatening complications.

Manage It, Master It

  • Monitor your blood pressure regularly
  • Eat heart-healthy meals
  • Exercise daily
  • Reduce stress with mindful practices
  • Follow doctor-prescribed medication plans

Consistent care and small changes can make a big difference in managing your blood pressure.
